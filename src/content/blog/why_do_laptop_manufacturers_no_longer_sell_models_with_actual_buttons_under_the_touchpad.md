---
title: Why do laptop manufacturers no longer sell models with actual buttons under
  the touchpad?
description: 'A rant from the perspective of someone doing serious work on laptops:
  physical touchpad buttons, proper arrow keys, and ergonomic design still matter.'
pubDate: '2024-08-12'
---

<p>These are sad times for anyone doing serious work (coding/writing/translating) on a laptop.</p>
<p>Why do companies keep removing features to make things sleeker and more minimalistic when it makes the computer less ergonomic? I spend an inordinate amount of time translating on my laptop, and I need:</p>

<ul>
<li>a touchpad with <strong>buttons under it</strong></li>
<li>properly sized up/down keys in a T-shaped navigation cluster (preferably separated from the rest of the keys, not squished in between them)</li></ul><p><img alt="The devolution of the laptop keyboard" src="./images/the-devolution-of-the-laptop-keyboard.jpg"></p>
<p>Why can't they keep at least one model for us professionals?</p>
<p>I'm currently stuck on my old-ish Dell Precision 7740 because all Dell's newer, so-called "workstation" models now have suboptim&nbsp;keyboards with messed-up arrow keys and no buttons below the touchpad.</p>
<p>I've used Dell workstations for around 15 years, but the 7740 will be my last Dell. It looks like I'm going to have to get a ThinkPad since Lenovo is one of the few companies left that still offers semi-decent keyboards.</p>
<p>I literally have £3,000 to spend on a work laptop, and Dell no longer offers a single professional model.</p>
